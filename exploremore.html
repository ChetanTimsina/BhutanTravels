Discover the Dzongkhags of Bhutan

Explore the beauty and diversity of Bhutan's 20 Dzongkhags, each with its own unique culture, landscapes, and history.

Paro

Paro is known for its breathtaking landscapes and home to Paro Taktsang, the Tiger's Nest Monastery.

Bumthang

Known for its beautiful valleys and sacred sites like Kurjey Lhakhang, Bumthang is a spiritual center.

Thimphu

The capital city of Bhutan, Thimphu blends modernity with tradition, offering cultural and natural beauty.

Trashigang

Located in eastern Bhutan, Trashigang is known for its picturesque landscapes and the Trashigang Dzong.

Zhemgang

Zhemgang is known for its biodiversity, rich cultural heritage, and stunning wildlife sanctuaries.

Samdrup Jongkhar

Located in the southeastern part of Bhutan, Samdrup Jongkhar is known for its subtropical climate and diverse culture.

Wangdue Phodrang

Famous for its annual Wangdue festival, this Dzongkhag is home to the Wangdue Dzong and stunning valleys.

Punakha

Located in the western part of Bhutan, Haa is known for its serene environment and beautiful mountain views.

Chhukha

Located near the Indian border, Chhukha is known for its mountainous landscapes and scenic valleys.
